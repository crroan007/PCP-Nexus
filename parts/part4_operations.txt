  <div class="section">
    <h2><span class="section-number">09</span> Phase 1 Workflow: Affidavits</h2>
    <p class="section-desc"><span class="pipeline-badge phase1">&#9679; Phase 1</span> &nbsp;One email in, one renamed PDF out.</p>
    <p>Each Tyler notification email for a civil process document is handled individually. The system downloads the PDF, applies the appropriate document-type rename code, and delivers the file to the network share.</p>
    <h3>Document Rename Map</h3>
    <p>Phase 1 applies court-compliant rename codes automatically:</p>
    <table>
      <thead><tr><th>Source Code</th><th>Output Code</th><th>Document Type</th></tr></thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr><td><code>AX02</code></td><td><code>AX42</code></td><td>Return of Service</td></tr>
        <tr><td><code>AX07</code></td><td><code>AX47</code></td><td>Attorney Motion</td></tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <p style="font-size: 13px; color: var(--gray-500);">Additional rename mappings follow the same pattern. Case numbers are extracted exactly as displayed in the source email.</p>
  </div>

  <span class="section-anchor" id="phase2-workflow"></span>
  <div class="section">
    <h2><span class="section-number">10</span> Phase 2 Workflow: Petitions</h2>
    <p class="section-desc"><span class="pipeline-badge phase2">&#9679; Phase 2</span> &nbsp;Multiple documents merge into one PDF.</p>
    <p>Petition filings arrive as multiple separate emails. PCP Nexus collects all related documents and merges them into a single PDF in a strict order.</p>
    <h3>Merge Order</h3>
    <ol class="steps">
      <li><div class="step-content"><strong>AX40 &#8212; Lead Document</strong><br>Always placed first in the merged PDF. Nothing merges until the Lead Document arrives.</div></li>
      <li><div class="step-content"><strong>Exhibits</strong><br>Placed in the middle of the merged document, in the order received.</div></li>
      <li><div class="step-content"><strong>Attachments</strong><br>Placed at the end of the merged PDF.</div></li>
    </ol>
    <h3>Excluded Document Types</h3>
    <p>The following are automatically filtered out and will <strong>not</strong> appear in the merged PDF:</p>
    <table>
      <thead><tr><th>Code</th><th>Reason</th></tr></thead>
      <tbody>
        <tr><td><code>AX69</code></td><td>Excluded by court rules</td></tr>
        <tr><td><code>AX81</code></td><td>Excluded by court rules</td></tr>
        <tr><td><code>CL</code> + digits</td><td>Clerk Letters &#8212; excluded from merge <span class="version-badge v44">v4.4</span></td></tr>
      </tbody>
    </table>
    <div class="callout new">
      <div class="callout-icon">&#127381;</div>
      <div class="callout-content">
        <strong>v4.4 Fix &#8212; Clerk Letter Detection</strong>
        <p>Filenames containing "CL" followed by digits (e.g., <code>AX40...CL2.pdf</code>) are now correctly identified as Clerk Letters and excluded from the merge. Earlier versions could miss this pattern.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
    <div class="callout tip">
      <div class="callout-icon">&#128161;</div>
      <div class="callout-content">
        <strong>Wait State</strong>
        <p>If exhibits or attachments arrive before the Lead Document (AX40), they are held in a queue. Merging only begins once the Lead Document is present.</p>
      </div>
    </div>
